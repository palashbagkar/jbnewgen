The India Tech Challenge

What global startups consistently get wrong about India's tech environment

India's digital infrastructure is not a smaller, slower version of the West. It is a parallel ecosystem with its own payment rails, identity systems, communication platforms, and regulatory mandates. Most global startups discover this after launch — which is exactly the wrong time.

💳

Your payment stack won't work as-is

Stripe and PayPal have limited reach in India. UPI processes over 10 billion transactions a month. If you're not integrated with India's payment rails, you're invisible to a huge segment of buyers.

⚖️

Data localisation is a legal requirement

India's DPDP Act and sector-specific RBI, IRDAI, and SEBI regulations require certain data categories to be stored within India. Launching without addressing this is a compliance risk from day one.

📱

Device and network fragmentation is real

India's users span flagship smartphones to entry-level Android devices, and 4G networks that drop to 2G in Tier 2 cities. Products not optimised for this range will underperform and churn.

🔁

Your CRM is built for the wrong sales motion

Indian B2B runs through distributors and resellers — not direct sales. A CRM configured for a direct sales motion will fail to track or manage India's channel-driven pipeline.

Cloud & Data Compliance for India

India's data protection landscape is evolving rapidly. The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, combined with sector-specific mandates from the RBI, IRDAI, SEBI, and MeitY, means that many global startups face mandatory data localisation, consent framework requirements, and breach notification obligations before they can legally onboard Indian customers. We assess your specific compliance exposure based on your product category and customer type, guide your cloud architecture decisions, and build the documentation and consent frameworks that regulators and enterprise customers in India will ask for.
